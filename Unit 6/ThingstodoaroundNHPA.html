    • Things to do around New Hope

    Go hiking at Goat Hill

    The hike up to the top of Goat Hill is quite unique, it's a pretty easy hike but has some steep parts along the way.
    The hike is beautiful and allows you to connect you the outdoors and nature. It gives you a break from the
    noisy and busy streets of downtown. Once you reach the top of Goat Hill you get a spectacular view of the Deleware River,
    the New Hope-Lambertville bridge, and New Hope its self. On top of the hill there are a couple picnic tables so you can eat lunch.
    If you like hiking an nature that I would highly suggest putting this in you plans when coming down to New Hope.

    Tubing down the Deleware River

    Obviously, this is only something you can only do when it's warm out. So doing this during the summer or maybe
    spring and fall if its warm enough would be a great idea. This is a great activity you can do with friends and family and is very relaxing.
    Just make sure to keep you but up because some of the rocks stick up out of the water. Also, for saftey reasons make sure that
    everyone is wearing their life vests at all times, as their are some tiny rapids in the river. If you do want to do this,
    I would suggest starting twenty minutes up the river from New Hope, around the Stockton area.
    This would give you about two hours, depeding on the speed of the current, in the water. All that being said,
    tubing down the river is a great activity to do and will be fun for all.

    Walk around Lambertville plus the bridge

    Being in New Hope you are bound to look across the river at some point. When you do, you will be looking at Lambertville in New Jersey!
    Taking the bridge over you can have two feet in two different states at the same time. Lambertville isn't as nice as New Hope,
    but still very nice. It has some nice places to eat just across the bridge and has easy access to the water.
    It has a wonderful hotel just as you cross over the bridge giving a great angle of New Hope, Lambertville, and the bridge.
    And its always fun to explore new things right!
